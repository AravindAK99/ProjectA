Calicut

Calicut is one of the best cities in Kerala. Endowed with lush green countrysides, serene beaches, historic sites, wildlife sanctuaries, rivers, and hills, this city enchants both its inhabitants and its guests. With its unique culture and friendly ambiance, Kozhikode is a wondrous destination for all.

When Zamorins ruled, Calicut was the capital of Malabar in northern Kerala. One should not miss out on the many tourist attractions that this destination offers. Moreover, this is the place where most of the spices are produced so this place is worth seeing at least once.

Best Food To Eat In Calicut

Some popular dishes include the Calicut Biriyani or the Malabar Biriyani, Ghee Rice with meat curry, a whole host of sea-food preparations (prawns, mussels, mackerel, sea-fish) and paper thin Pathiris to provide accompaniment to spicy gravy.

Best Time To Visit Calicut

It is the best time to visit Kozhikode for sightseeing. June to September is the monsoon season. The city experiences heavy rainfall in the season. To enjoy the festivals and fairs of the city, one can visit the place during August and September.

Top Destionations In Calicut

1. Kappad beach:

Kappad Beach has historically played an important role in the history of Kerala. On these shores, over 500 years ago in 1498, 170 men led by Vasco- da-Gama first stepped onto Kerala. The entire State, especially the Malabar Coast, would never be the same again.

A visit to Calicut is incomplete without a visit to this legendary site. The spice route flourished through this Beach. A walk in and around the place gives one an idea of itshistorical relevance. The rocks and small hills add to its charm. The nearby shacks are always stocked with excellent local delicacies and tea. Migratory birds can be spotted occasionally as well. Kappad Beach is a truly majestic destination, which will always hold an important place in our history.

2. Beypore Beach:

Beypore Beach is situated at the mouth of the Chaliyar River, 10 km from Kozhikode Railway Station. Now an important port and fishing port, this place was also an important commercial and maritime centre during the days of early Arab and Chinese travellers and Europeans.

Beypore Beach is a commercial centre for the construction of ships and has been in existence here for more than 1000 years. This shipyard is famous for making a distinctive ship known as ‘Uru.’ One of the main attractions of Beypore is its magnificent 2 km long stone bridge called Pulimoodu, made entirely of stones, which leads to the sea. It is also one of the main tourist attractions in Calicut

3. Thali Temple:

Thali Temple is a 14th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva located in the heart of Kozhikode City, 1 km from Kozhikode Railway Station.

This temple was destructed by Tippu Sultan and Hyder Ali during their invasion of Kozhikode and was later renovated and rebuilt by Swami Thirumulapad, the then Zamorin of Calicut. The two-story sanctuary features artistic stone sculptures and wood carvings with scenes from the Puranas. Very high walls and an expansive temple pond are some of the special features of this temple. The temple complex also houses the shrine of Lord Krishna.
