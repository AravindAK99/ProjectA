Thrissur ("Cultural capital of Kerala")

Thrissur is a land full of festivals and rituals that liven up the city all year round. There is not a single dull moment here. The Vadakkunnathan Temple, one of Asia’s largest temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, is located in Thrissur. The Bible Tower, which is one of the tallest towers in Asia, is also situated here. The Thrissur Zoo and Museum is another tourist attraction in the city. There is also an archeological museum, which is nothing short of fascinating, and the Shakthan Thampuran Palace that you can visit in Thrissur.

Chavakkad Beach, Peechi Dam and Charpa Falls are perfect places to truly soak in the city’s natural beauty. A visit to any of these spots is sure to be a relaxing and rejuvenating one. The Vilangan Hils located here provide panoramic views of the city and the surrounding, making it a great picnic spot or couple’s getaway.

Best Food To Eat In Thrissur

The Thrissur food is celebrated the world over these days. Due to the tremendous tourist influx in recent years not only have the residents of Thrissur begun serving up a delectable repast but their own eating habits have changed. Dosai, Idli, Poori, Chapati along with tea and coffee. Fish and tapioca form a large part of the diet of the people of Thrissur. Another staple additive to the daily food is the utilization of large quantities of grated coconut as well as coconut milk. Most of the dishes are prepared with the vital ingredients like of fragrant spices found in abundance in this region. Cardamom, ginger, black pepper, cloves and cinnamon to name a few.

Best Time To Visit Thrissur

The best time to visit Thrissur is the season of winter when tourists will find the climate on the pleasant and enjoyable side. During this season, you can take pleasure in the outdoor visits as well as sightseeing. The city experiences the monsoon season from June and remains till September.

Top Destionations In Thrissur

1. Athirappally Waterfalls:

One of the famous tourist attractions in Kerala, the magnificent waterfalls are a sight worth witnessing. This is the biggest waterfall in Kerala. Athirapally falls comes down from a height of 80 feet through several parallel streams. In the monsoon season, in its full might, the waterfalls appear like Niagara Falls.

Apart from Athirapally, there are two other waterfalls that one must visit like Chapa and Vazhachal waterfalls. One can even go on a tour to nearby hill stations to enjoy the breathtaking beauty of the Western Ghats, laden in the green blanket of nature.

2. Chavakkad Beach:

Chavakkad Beach in Thrissur is famous for the Azhimukam, the confluence of the river and sea. Visitors throng here to observe this delightful phenomenon. It is a great place to swim and relax in a serene environment. The nearby lighthouse attracts frequent visitors as well. On the other side of the beach is Ramachapadam, a vast farmland of the Ayurvedic herb, Ramacham. The fish market available in the evening sees a huge crowd every day. It is among the best picnic spots in Thrissur.
